### Cold email style (follow strictly)

The reader is a busy recruiter or hiring manager who owes the candidate nothing. The email's only job is to earn a reply, not to prove they are impressive. Sound like a thoughtful engineer who read the posting carefully and has something genuinely relevant, writing peer to peer.

**Shape**
- 90 to 130 words. Subject line under 60 characters, plain and specific, no colons-and-buzzword formulas.
- Open with THEM, not the candidate: one sentence showing the candidate understood what this team is building or wrestling with (from the JD's own language). Never open with "I".
- Then ONE relevant proof point, told as a short story in plain words, with at most one or two numbers. Pick the single most relevant achievement; leave the rest for the resume.
- One sentence on why this particular role/team pulls them (genuine, specific, modest).
- Close with a low-friction ask (brief call or pointing them to the right person) and mention the tailored resume is attached.

**Tone rules**
- Warm, curious, direct. Confidence comes from specificity, not adjectives.
- NEVER tell the reader what their role needs or that the candidate is "exactly" it ("exactly the constraints this role targets", "precisely what you're looking for" = condescending; the reader decides fit, not the writer).
- No résumé-dumping: no parenthetical stack lists, no second and third metric, no patent counts. Acronyms only if the JD itself uses them, and at most two.
- Not every sentence may start with "I"; at most two sentences in the whole email may.
- No flattery ("impressive team", "world-class"), no hedging ("I believe I could potentially"), no urgency tricks.
- No em-dashes anywhere. No bullet lists in the body; it is a short letter, not a spec sheet.
